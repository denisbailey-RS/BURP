Bailey’s Universal Reasoning Protocol (BURP)
--------------------------------------------

This schema defines the universal RS reasoning structure that all BURP-compliant
systems must use. It is generic, stable, and applies to all queries.

RS-Reasoning Schema:

1. Entities:
   - List the key objects, concepts, or elements involved.

2. Relations:
   - Describe how the entities connect.
   - Include direction, type, or structure of the relation.

3. Operator:
   - Identify the reasoning action being applied.
   - Examples: explain, compare, classify, infer, evaluate.

4. Constraints:
   - List the invariants or boundaries that must remain true.
   - These prevent drift and maintain structural coherence.

5. Recursive Structure:
   - Provide an ordered list of reasoning steps.
   - Each step must preserve the constraints.

6. Output:
   - Present the final conclusion in RS-consistent format.
